Rounds Explained

This tutorial page explains the 4 different types of rounds and their uses for the experiments.

Text rounds just contain text. They are the simplest type of round.

Subjects are played audios of words spoken by the researcher. These words are paired together, and in later rounds the subjects are expected to recall the second word spoken in a pair, when prompted with the first word. Along with this, there is added background noises and music played. When creating an audio round, default audios or the user's audios can be specified, along with a number of pairs.

Image rounds contain images that are presented to the user. They also contain an accompanying question with different answer types. (slider bar, input box, yes or no, agree)

Survey rounds are used by the researcher to collect user feedback. The experiment creator specifies a question and also an answer type, similar to the image round.
